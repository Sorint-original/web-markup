The start of change in art



            About a century ago, in the early 1900s Marcel Duchamp was an artist associated with the Dadaism movement, which was a movement of art that expressed the early idea that the world is without reason, but rather, it is irrational and absurd. In 1917 Duchamp created an art piece called “Fountain” , which was essentially just a urinal he purchased from a plumbing store. At first it was rejected when he attempted to exhibit it as art, but after some time, it would become accepted and redeemed as an ingenious and highly influential piece. In these piece, Duchamp put the question of what art could be, and whether or not it had to be made by the artist or if it could be merely re-contextualization or an addition of what already existed.
