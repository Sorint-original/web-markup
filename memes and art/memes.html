            Like many other art forms of art in recent history, memes make use of the everyday objects and people in culture. This items and people are then recontextualized and distributed freely across the internet for everyone to partake and share personal meaning, relevance, and experience through.



            In a postmodern culture where meaning is derived increasingly from individual experience, focused through and into the internet, we are perhaps experiencing a sort of strange development of a post-modern digital art based on shared participation.
